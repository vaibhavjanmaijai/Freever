The Philosophy

Why should love be a life sentence?

Humans change. Needs evolve. The idea that two strangers who fall in love at 24 must be perfectly compatible at 44, 64, and beyond — and that separating them is a failure — is a modern myth built on old institutions.

Choose Again proposes something radical: that commitment can be intentional and renewable. That choosing to stay is more meaningful than being legally trapped to stay. That two adults can define the shape, duration, and terms of their partnership — together.

This isn't about avoiding commitment. It's about committing consciously.

01

Intentional Renewal

Every term end is a checkpoint — a moment to reflect, grow, and actively choose to continue. Staying together becomes a choice, not a default.

02

Dignity in Separation

When a term ends without renewal, both parties part without stigma. No failure, no scandal — just two lives that ran their beautiful course together.

03

Defined from the Start

Financial terms, living arrangements, shared responsibilities — all agreed upon upfront, reducing conflict and building clarity from day one.

04

Equal Agency

Both partners hold identical rights. No gender-based defaults. No cultural pressure. The contract reflects what you two decide — nothing more.

CHOOSE AGAIN PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT

A Voluntary, Time-Bound, Mutually Consented Partnership Contract

"We, the undersigned, enter this partnership of our own free will, in full awareness of its terms, duration, and conditions. We affirm that love expressed through clarity and consent is stronger than love maintained by obligation alone."
Article I

Definitions & Parties

This Agreement is entered into between Partner A and Partner B, both adults of sound mind, unmarried or legally free to enter this contract, who mutually consent to a defined partnership for the duration specified herein.

Article II

Term Duration

  • The initial partnership term shall be [1 / 2 / 3 / 5] year(s) from the date of signing.
  • The term may be renewed, renegotiated, or allowed to expire — decided jointly 90 days prior to expiry.
  • Either party may call for early termination with 60 days written notice and mutual mediation.
  • Renewal is not automatic. Both parties must actively affirm continuation.
Article III

Living Arrangements

  • Cohabitation arrangements are specified at signing: shared residence, separate residences, or hybrid.
  • Both parties retain individual rights to their pre-existing property.
  • Jointly acquired assets during the term are governed by the Shared Asset Clause (Article VI).
Article IV

Financial Terms

  • Each partner's financial independence is fully preserved.
  • Shared expenses (rent, utilities, travel) shall be split as specified: [equally / proportional to income / other].
  • Neither party shall be financially liable for the other's pre-existing debts.
  • In the event of non-renewal, no spousal maintenance is owed unless explicitly agreed in a separate addendum.
Article V

Exclusivity & Personal Boundaries

  • The nature of exclusivity (monogamous / non-monogamous / to be defined) is agreed upon at signing.
  • Both partners retain full autonomy over their bodies, careers, social lives, and personal identities.
  • Neither party shall restrict the other's freedom of movement, association, or expression.
Article VI

Shared Assets & Property

  • Any property purchased jointly during the term is co-owned 50/50 unless otherwise specified.
  • Upon expiry without renewal, jointly held assets shall be divided per the Asset Division Addendum.
  • Sentimental or personal items remain with the original owner at all times.
Article VII

Children & Family Planning

  • Decision on children is a separate, explicit mutual agreement — never assumed.
  • If a child is born during the term, the contract automatically extends for custody and parenting clarity.
  • Both parents retain equal rights and obligations regardless of partnership status.
Article VIII

Dispute Resolution

  • Disputes shall first be addressed through structured mediation by a neutral third party.
  • Legal proceedings are a last resort, pursued only after mediation is exhausted.
  • Both parties agree to engage in good faith at all stages of resolution.
Article IX

Non-Renewal & Separation

  • If either party chooses not to renew, the partnership concludes at term end without penalty.
  • Both parties agree to cooperate in the orderly transition of shared responsibilities.
  • No social stigma, cultural blame, or legal consequence attaches to peaceful non-renewal.
  • A 30-day "transition window" post-expiry is available for logistical separation.
Article X

Mutual Consent Affirmation

Both parties confirm that this agreement is entered freely, without coercion, fully informed, and in alignment with their values. This contract represents their chosen architecture of love and partnership.

The Story Behind It

Built from a question no one wanted to ask

"What if we stopped pretending forever was the only measure of love?"

Growing up, Vaibhav Kumar watched people around him enter marriages not out of deep choice, but out of obligation — family pressure, social expectation, the quiet fear of being judged. He saw couples who were genuinely good people, simply incompatible, staying together because leaving carried shame heavier than staying.

At 26, after watching a close friend go through a marriage that dissolved in silence, Vaibhav asked a question that most found uncomfortable: what if the institution isn't broken — what if the expectations we've layered onto it are?

He spent two years reading philosophy, family law across multiple countries, and countless real stories of couples who found their own way to love. The conclusion was clear — people don't need less commitment. They need better-designed commitment. Commitment that has honesty built in. Commitment that respects the reality that human beings change.

Choose Again was born from that belief. Not as a cynical rejection of love — but as its deepest possible affirmation. Every time two people renew their agreement, they're saying something far more powerful than "I'm still here because I have to be." They're saying: I choose you again, today, fully aware of who you are and who I've become.

That is the kind of love Vaibhav set out to build a platform for. And that is the kind of love you deserve.

Questions

Common questions

Is this legally recognized? +
In many countries, cohabitation agreements and domestic partnership contracts hold legal weight. We work with legal professionals to ensure your contract is as enforceable as possible within your jurisdiction. Traditional marriage registration is separate and optional.
What if one person wants to renew but the other doesn't? +
Renewal requires both parties' consent. If one partner chooses not to renew, the contract expires at term end. No one can be compelled to continue. This is by design — honoring both people's agency fully.
Can we convert this to a traditional marriage later? +
Absolutely. Many couples use Choose Again as a conscious first phase, and after one or more successful renewals, choose to convert to a traditional legal marriage. The two are not mutually exclusive.
What happens if we have children during the term? +
A child's wellbeing always takes precedence. Upon pregnancy or birth, a Child & Custody Addendum automatically supplements the contract, ensuring both parents' rights and responsibilities are clearly defined regardless of the partnership's future.
Is Choose Again anti-marriage or anti-religion? +
Not at all. Choose Again is pro-choice — we believe people should have more options, not fewer. Traditional marriage remains a beautiful institution for those it serves. Choose Again simply offers an alternative for those whose values, circumstances, or philosophy call for something different.
Can same-sex or non-binary couples use Choose Again? +
Yes, absolutely and without exception. Choose Again is designed for any two adults entering a partnership. The contract uses neutral language and makes no assumptions about gender, sexuality, or relationship structure.
